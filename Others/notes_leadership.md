# Leadership Notes

## Beginner

- Leadership in a technical career is about helping teams succeed.
- Key skills:
  - communication
  - planning
  - accountability
- Build trust by being reliable and transparent.
- Understand stakeholder needs and keep them informed.

## Intermediate

- Technical leadership:
  - guide design decisions and technical direction
  - mentor junior engineers
  - help the team choose the right tools and approaches
- Stakeholder communication:
  - translate technical concepts into business outcomes
  - report progress clearly and honestly
  - surface risks early and propose mitigations
- Estimation:
  - break work into smaller tasks
  - estimate effort and duration conservatively
  - update estimates as new information arrives
- Mentoring:
  - provide constructive feedback
  - teach by example and encourage learning
  - pair program or review code together

## Advanced

- Architecture reviews:
  - evaluate design trade-offs and technical risk
  - check scalability, reliability, and maintainability
  - ensure security and cost implications are considered
- Leadership behaviors:
  - foster psychological safety so people can ask questions
  - support team autonomy while aligning to goals
  - help resolve conflicts and unblock team members
- Strategic thinking:
  - balance short-term delivery with long-term technical health
  - advocate for investment in quality and automation
  - align engineering work with business priorities

## Practical advice

- Practice active listening in meetings.
- Keep documentation accurate and easy to find.
- Use retrospective feedback to improve processes.
- Share knowledge through demos, docs, and coaching.
